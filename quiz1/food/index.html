Local Food Specialties

Kue Rangi

Kue Rangi is another traditional Betawi delicacy that is also a specialty of Bekasi. Its shape is quite unique, curved in a half-circle with a delicious filling. Made from grated coconut and tapioca flour, Kue Rangi is cooked in a special mold and then topped with palm sugar syrup.

Kue Biji Ketapang

Still in the form of cookies, this dish is a favorite among the people of Bekasi. Kue Biji Ketapang is also a result of Betawi cultural influence in Bekasi. The texture of Kue Biji Ketapang is slightly hard on the outside, but savory when eaten. Kue Biji Ketapang is made from sago mixed with coconut milk and sugar.

Kue Dongkal

Switching to traditional market snacks, kue dongkal is a food that is also rarely found. Dodongkal or Dongkal is a traditional Indonesian cake made from finely ground rice flour. The fine rice flour is then filled with palm sugar and steamed.

Kue Sagon

Still in the cake category, Kue Sagon is a classic dish from Bekasi that is also a typical Betawi food. Kue Sagon is made from a mixture of grated coconut and sago flour, which can also be replaced with glutinous rice flour or tapioca flour. In addition, Kue Sagon is usually topped with grated coconut.
